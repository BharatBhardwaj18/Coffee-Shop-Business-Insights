#  Coffee Shop Business Insights — Excel Dashboard


I made this project to practice data analysis using Excel.
The idea was to take real coffee shop sales data and figure out
what the business is doing well and where it can improve.

Everything is inside one interactive dashboard — you can filter by month,
by day, and all the charts update on their own.

## Tools Used

Microsoft Excel — Pivot Tables, Charts, and Slicers

## Data Overview

The dataset covers 6 months of sales from January to June
across 3 store locations — Astoria, Hell's Kitchen, and Lower Manhattan.

Total customers who visited — 1,49,116
Total revenue in 6 months — $6,98,812
Average bill per customer — $4.69
Average items per order — 1.4

## What I Analyzed

**Sales by day and hour**
Monday and Friday are the busiest days of the week.
Sundays are the slowest. Hourly data showed that 9 to 10 AM
is the peak time with the highest number of orders — this is
the morning rush when people grab coffee before work.
After 7 PM the shop gets very quiet.

**Store wise performance**
All three stores earn similar revenue which was interesting to see.
Hell's Kitchen has the most customers and the highest total sales.
Lower Manhattan has fewer customers but earns almost the same as the others —
which means customers there spend more per visit.

**What customers spend**
On average a customer spends $4.69 and orders around 1 to 2 items per visit.
Usually one coffee and maybe a snack on the side.

**Best selling products**
Barista Espresso is the top product by a big margin.
After that — Brewed Chai Tea, Gourmet Brewed Coffee,
Hot Chocolate, and Brewed Black Tea.

**Category wise sales**
Coffee brings in 39% of total revenue.
Tea is second at 28%.
Together they make up 67% of all sales —
so these two categories are the heart of the business.

## Key Takeaways

Morning 9 to 10 AM is the most important time slot for this business.
Weekdays perform better than weekends — the customer base is mostly office crowd.
Barista Espresso alone drives a large chunk of revenue.
Lower Manhattan customers have higher spending power even with less footfall.
Coffee and Tea together make up most of the money — everything else is secondary.

## Dashboard Features

Month filter and Day filter — select any time period and everything updates.
4 KPI cards at the top showing live totals for sales, customers, avg bill and avg orders.
6 charts covering hourly trends, weekday patterns, store comparison,
top products, category split, and size distribution.

 Built with Excel as part of my data analysis learning.
